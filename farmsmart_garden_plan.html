02 // Watering Zones

Eight relays, eight circuits.

The 8-channel relay bank on the Pi maps cleanly to 8 watering circuits — one per water-need cohort. Each zone gets its own moisture threshold, schedule, and drip geometry. Bed zones (Z1–Z4) run drip line along rows; pot zones (Z5–Z8) use one emitter per pot.

Z1DRIP · 2× / WK

Bed 1 · NW row

Targets: B1Q1–Q4 · Okra ×4 + Brinjal ×2
Deep infrequent watering. Tall plants — taproots push down for water.
Z2DRIP · 4× / WK

Bed 1 · SE row

Targets: B1Q5–Q8 · Basil, Beetroot, Radish, Brinjal
Consistent moisture — root crops split if watered unevenly.
Z3DRIP · 2× / WK

Bed 2 · NW row

Targets: B2Q1–Q4 · Tomato ×4 (caged)
Deep soak every 3–4 days. Pushes roots deep, concentrates sugar in fruit.
Z4DRIP · 3× / WK

Bed 2 · SE row

Targets: B2Q5–Q8 · Roselle, Basil, Beet+Radish intercrop
Medium moisture. Roselle tolerates some drought; intercrop needs steady water.
Z5EMITTER · DAILY

Bottle Gourd pots

Targets: BG1, BG2, BG3 · 3 emitters
Highest water demand in the plan. Daily in peak summer, twice daily if ≥90°F.
Z6EMITTER · DAILY

Cucumber + Zucchini pots

Targets: CU1, CU2, ZC1, ZC2 · 4 emitters
Cucurbits need consistent moisture. Pots dry fast — never let them fully dry.
Z7EMITTER · EVERY 2 DAYS

Capsicum pots

Targets: CP1–CP4 · 4 emitters
Peppers prefer slightly dry cycles — overwatering causes blossom drop.
Z8EMITTER · EVERY 2 DAYS

Green Chili pots

Targets: GC1–GC6 · 6 emitters
Similar schedule to capsicum. Stress cycles actually concentrate capsaicin.

05 // Pot Strategy

Seventeen pots, five playbooks.

Pots are the flexible layer — move them with the sun, pull them away from pests. Five roles split cleanly into three watering zones.

3 POTS · Z520 GAL · ≥16" DEEP

Bottle Gourd · Fence climbers

Aggressive 20-ft vines. Kept in pots so roots stay contained; trained up three different fences for max fruiting surface.

BG1 SW corner
BG2 SE middle
BG3 NE corner
SOIL fresh mix
  • Run jute twine or 4" netting up each fence section.
  • Support heavy fruits with cloth slings.
2 POTS · Z610 GAL · ≥14" DEEP

Zucchini · Bush variety

Moved from beds to pots. Use a bush cultivar (Black Beauty, Raven) that stays compact.

ZC1 top strip
ZC2 top strip
MIX compost-heavy
FEED every 2 wks
  • Hand-pollinate early morning if fruit set is poor — male flowers open first.
  • Heavy feeder — add organic fertilizer at planting.
2 POTS · Z65 GAL · ≥12" DEEP

Cucumber · SE-fence climbers

Moved from beds to pots along the SE fence. Trellised vertically up the fence for clean fruit and airflow.

CU1 left of BG2
CU2 right of BG2
TRELLIS SE fence
PAIR with dill
  • Use bush or compact vining varieties (Spacemaster, Salad Bush).
  • Pinch laterals above 5th node for stronger upward growth.
4 POTS · Z75 GAL · ≥12" DEEP

Capsicum · Wall-banked peppers

Bell peppers set fruit best at 70–85°F. Two pots against the house wall bank heat; two in the SE strip catch sun.

CP1 / CP2 NW strip
CP3 / CP4 SE strip
STAKE 3 ft bamboo
FEED + bone meal
  • Keep pots 2 ft apart minimum for airflow.
  • Mulch pot surface with straw.
6 POTS · Z83 GAL · ≥10" DEEP

Green Chili · Scattered heat-lovers

Six pots spread across both strips — chilies are prolific, and spreading them across pots spreads the risk.

GC1–GC3 NW strip
GC4–GC6 SE strip
PAIR with basil
FEED monthly
  • Basil seedling in each chili pot — deters aphids and whiteflies.
  • Pinch early flowers for the first 4–6 weeks.
06 // IoT Stack · Next up

Ready for the automation layer.

Plan is locked. Time to wire it up.
